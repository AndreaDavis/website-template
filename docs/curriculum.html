Digital Publishing

This session will introduce participants to two digital publishing platforms— Scalar and Omeka. Scalar and Omeka are two of the most commonly-used free and open-source publishing platforms for scholarly communication. In addition to learning the basics and basic differences of each platform, participants will begin to build a project on one of the platforms. This workshop is geared towards beginners – no prior experience with either publishing platform is necessary.

Digital Design

This session will introduce attendees to two mark-up languages- HTML and CSS. These are two of the most commonly-used languages in rendering information on the web today. In addition to learning the basics and basic differences of each language, participants will apply best practices in digital design by customizing a Scalar or Omeka project using HTML and CSS. Beyond learning HTML and CSS, users will leave the workshop with a clearer understanding of how the internet works. This workshop is geared towards beginners – no prior experience with either language or website-building is necessary.

Ethics

This discussion-based workshop will address an array of ethical questions and concerns for community-based research and teaching. In addressing issues of confidentiality, inclusion, participation, and accessibility, this workshop will first provide a general overview of ethics for institutional research compliance —including the Belmont Report and Institutional Review Board — and then delve into ethical issues that extend beyond institutional purview.

Project Planning

What separates projects that turn into something from those that stall out and go nowhere is the formulation (and constant revision and adaptation) of a reasonable, informed, and purposeful project plan. During this session, participants will be introduced to sound project development and management practices. Starting with an end goal in mind, an articulation of the needs and opportunities, collaborators, audience, resources, and work plan, participants will draft a one to three page proposal for their DH projects.

Project Lab

This session will provide participants with time to work independently or—if desired—in groups to begin designing their own digital heritage projects for research or teaching. Instructors will be on hand to answer questions and provide feedback.

Presentations

Each participant will present a five-minute proposal for a digital heritage project.
