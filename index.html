What is the Digital Humanities Research Institute?

The Digital Humanities Research Institute (DHRI) is a four-day workshop held from X-X May 2019 at the A-State Faculty Center. Focused on the theme “Heritage in the Digital Age,” DHRI builds on college-wide interest and expertise in Heritage Studies to create a community of digital humanities practice at Arkansas State University. During the workshop, which is supported by the Graduate Center Digital Initiatives at CUNY and the National Endowment for the Humanities, participants will explore digital humanities research and pedagogy, develop core computational skills through hands-on workshops, and begin designing their own digital heritage projects for research or teaching.

Reasons to Participate

Build Community

Engage with colleagues who are passionate about using digital tools to enhance their work.

Learn New Skills

Build technical and professional skills through collaborative and group-oriented workshops.

Share Resources

Learn how to teach, adapt, and share digital projects and methodologies with others.
